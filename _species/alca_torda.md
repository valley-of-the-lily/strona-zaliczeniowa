The razorbill (*Alca torda*) is a seabird belonging to the auk family (*Alcidae*). It inhabits the northern Atlantic Ocean and breeds on rocky cliffs and islands in regions such as Iceland, the British Isles, Scandinavia, and eastern Canada. The species is closely related to the extinct great auk and is well adapted to life at sea.

### Key Characteristics of *Alca torda*

* **Appearance:**
  * Medium-sized seabird, typically measuring **38–43 cm** in length.
  * Wingspan ranges from **60 to 69 cm**.
  * Weighs around **500–900 g**.
  * Black upperparts and head with a white underside.
  * Thick, laterally compressed black bill with a thin white line.
  * During the breeding season, a narrow white line extends from the eye to the bill.

* **Behavior:**
  * Highly adapted to marine life and spends most of the year at sea.
  * Excellent diver, capable of reaching depths of **up to about 100 meters**.
  * Uses its wings to “fly” underwater while hunting.
  * Usually nests in large colonies on steep cliffs.
  * Outside the breeding season it lives mostly alone or in small groups at sea.

* **Diet:**
  * Primarily feeds on **small fish** such as herring, sand eels, and sprats.
  * Also eats crustaceans and other small marine organisms.
  * Catches prey by diving and chasing fish underwater.

* **Habitat:**
  * Coastal marine environments of the **North Atlantic Ocean**.
  * Breeds on **rocky cliffs, offshore islands, and steep coastal ledges**.
  * Prefers cold ocean waters rich in fish.

### Conservation Status and Threats

* **IUCN Status:** Classified as **Least Concern**.

* **Threats:**
  * **Oil spills:** Pollution in the ocean can damage feathers and reduce survival.
  * **Climate change:** Changes in ocean temperature can affect fish availability.
  * **Overfishing:** Reduces the number of fish available for feeding.
  * **Human disturbance:** Tourism or activity near breeding cliffs may disturb colonies.

### Reproduction

* Breeding season occurs in **spring and early summer**.
* The species nests in **dense colonies** on cliffs and rocky islands.
* Females typically lay **one egg** directly on bare rock or in small crevices.
* Both parents share incubation duties for about **35–37 days**.
* After hatching, the chick is fed by both parents until it leaves the nest and heads to sea with the male parent.
